Part I: Definition of Streaming

Streaming can be defined as the playback of a multimedia file that can be played without the need to download it, with the use of the internet. There are 2 types of streaming media:

  • On-Demand Streaming

    • It allows the audience or viewers to view their selected content whenever they want, with the ability to pause, fast-forward, rewind, or otherwise manipulate the media they're consuming.

  • Live Streaming

    • On the other hand, live streaming the opposite of the above, because whereas content for On-Demand Streaming is pre-recorded, content for Live Streaming is broadcasted in real time, hence the word "Live".

    • Usually, content for live Live Streaming cannot be played backwards, but some platforms for consuming said content allows for it.

b. Music Video

A music video is a videotaped performance of a recorded popular song, usually accompanied by dancing and visual images interpreting the lyrics. Although the origins of music videos began with “musical short films” that first appeared in the 1920s, they really came into prominence in the 1980s when MTV created their channel around the medium and it changed how we received our music.[14]

According to a survey conducted in Italy in 2020, more women than men listened to music every day in their free time. As reported by the data, 38 percent of female respondents claimed to listen to music every day. By contrast, 35 percent of men did the same as of the survey period. Moreover, 13 percent of female interviewees said to never listen to music in their free time. Conversely, 10 percent of male respondents claimed to not listen to music at all as of 2020.[15]

In 2015, online music videos in the United Kingdom started getting age ratings from the British Board of Film Classification (BBFC) in the same way films do, following a UK Government pilot. Of the 132 videos submitted to the BBFC so far, 56 are rated 12 and 53 classified 15. Only one video got an 18 certificate - Dizzee Rascal's Couple Of Stack.[16]

c. Twitch

Twitch is a live-streaming platform for gamers and other lifestyle casters that supports building communities around a shared and streamable interest.

Twitch streamers "broadcast" their gameplay or activity by sharing their screen with fans and subscribers who can hear and watch them live.

There are both free and paid versions of Twitch, with paid tiers, known as Twitch Prime, removing ads and giving users access to more robust social, streaming, and storage features.

  • Twitch users consume 71 million hours of content on a daily basis.
  • Twitch sees an average of 15 million daily active users (DAUs).
  • Over 2.2 million Twitch creators broadcast their games live on Twitch monthly.
  • Twitch’s Partner Program has grown by 68% since 2017.
  • Twitch viewers consumed 18.6 billion hours of streamed video content in 2020.
  • Twitch’s peak viewership sits at 6.5 million views.
  • Twitch has 140 million unique monthly visitors.
  • Twitch has 9.2 million monthly active streamers.
  • Daily active users spend an average of 95 minutes on Twitch.
  • Twitch’s core demographic consists of 65% male users, 73% of which are under 35.

According to statistics, the United States uses Twitch the most with 22.67% of Twitch's desktop traffic worldwide coming from it, followed by Germany with 6.73%.[17][18][19]

Part V: Need of Streaming Viewers

The specific audience that we, as a group, have selected to analyse, is the people who play the 1st person shooting game VALORANT. Below are the recursive questions we asked in order to determine the common need among the aforementioned audience:

Why do people watch Valorant streams?

  • So people can learn a different variety of skills from the game.

Why do people want to learn a different variety of skills from the game?

  • Because people want to improve in playing Valorant.

Why do people want to improve in Valorant?

  • Because they want to have a higher rank.

Why do people want to have a higher rank?

  • So they can enjoy the game more.

Why do people want to enjoy the game more?

  • So they can get entertained.

Why do people want to get entertained in the first place?

  • To avoid boredom or relieve stress.

From this, we have determined that as for most things, the root cause for people watching Valorant streams in the first place is to relieve boredom, which is an understandably human need.

We asked more additional recursive questions to further specify what the audience needs:

What can they gain from watching Valorant Streams?

  • People can learn new skills and improve the skills they have from watching other people, especially those who are more skilled in the game.

How will they watch Valorant streams?

  • By using platforms like Facebook, Youtube ,and Twitch.

Why is Valorant popular?

  • It's free to play. Perhaps the most compelling reason why Filipinos have gotten into Valorant is because it is completely free.It is also similar to another popular shooter game, Counter Strike: Global Offensive, but with a twist, putting agents with different kinds of mechanics, skills, and new maps as well.

From this, we can determine that the core need of those who watch Valorant streams is simply to know all the basic skills that are needed to know how to play, and improve playing, Valorant.

The stream should be entertaining and interactive, and it should always follow the hype for better exposure. But always following the hype isn't always good, because if there is one thing people hate, it is repetitive content.
